📖 ROK GLOSSARY — PLAIN ENGLISH

What all these terms actually mean
ROK Score (0-100)
How confident ROK is in a signal. 80+ = very strong. 60+ = good. Below 60 = use caution.
Stop Loss
The price where you should sell if the trade goes wrong. Like a seatbelt — it limits how much you can lose.
Price Target
Wall Street analysts' best guess for where a stock is headed. Not a guarantee, but useful as a goal.
Risk/Reward (R:R)
Compares potential loss to potential gain. 1:2 means risking $1 to make $2. Higher is better.
RSI (Momentum)
Measures if a stock is being bought or sold too aggressively. Above 70 = maybe too hot. Below 30 = possibly cheap.
Volume Spike
Today's trading volume vs the 30-day average. 2x+ means unusual interest — hedge funds or institutions are moving.
Congressional Buy
A US senator or representative disclosed buying this stock with their own money. Required by law within 45 days.
Insider Buy (Form 4)
A company executive bought their own company's stock. Insiders only buy with personal money when they expect it to go up.
Unusual Options
A large fund placed a big bet that this stock moves significantly. These trades often cost $1M+.
Short Squeeze
When lots of traders bet a stock falls, but it goes up instead, forcing them to all buy at once — causing a rapid price spike.
Fear & Greed Index
CNN measures overall market emotion. High fear (under 30) often means stocks are cheap. High greed (over 70) = be careful.
Put/Call Ratio
Measures how many bets hedge funds are making on stocks going down vs up. Below 0.8 = bullish. Above 1.2 = bearish.
